// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project images—Lincoln City brownfield assessments, the OSU-Cascades former landfill conversion in Bend, the Detroit Marinas excavation in Marion County—are almost certainly deployed as unnamed, untagged decorative files with no alt-text, no JSON-LD Project schema, and no geotagged EXIF data tying them to specific jurisdictions. A procurement officer at a Pacific Northwest port authority searching "brownfield redevelopment consultant Oregon" is landing on a competitor's page because that competitor's project photography is functioning as indexed, locatable evidence and yours is invisible to the crawl. Every untagged site photo is a dead asset in a content library that should be doing structural work.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your real estate developer and attorney clients are making six- and seven-figure engagement decisions, and your site gives them zero visual documentation of actual fieldwork, deliverable samples, or team-in-context photography. You claim "dedicated advocates" and "exceptional results" but provide no images of your people on a remediation site, no GIS output visuals, no before/after of a brownfields conversion—the exact proof layer that a cautious legal or real estate client requires before picking up the phone. Written claims without visual corroboration read as unverifiable to a high-stakes buyer who has been burned before.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Firms like Landau Associates and Parametrix are running project-specific visual case study pages with annotated site photography, team headshots embedded in project context, and mapped service-area graphics that signal Pacific Northwest geographic depth instantly. You are serving Jefferson County, Pierce County, Marion County, and Lincoln City simultaneously but presenting zero visual geographic differentiation—no site photography, no map assets, no jurisdiction-specific imagery—which means a local agency procurement team comparing proposals perceives you as generic rather than embedded, and awards the relationship to the firm that looks like it already belongs there.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured field documentation protocol across your active project types—brownfield excavation, community planning sessions, GIS data deliverable outputs, and fleet electrification site visits—capturing assets that are immediately tagged at ingestion with location coordinates, project type, client sector, and service line using a standardized IPTC/XMP metadata schema before a single file hits your CMS. Each image is then deployed with hand-written alt-text structured as `[service] + [location] + [client sector]`, JSON-LD `Project` markup on every case study page, and filename conventions that match high-intent search strings your buyers actually use. This converts your photography from a design decoration layer into a crawlable, indexed, trust-building infrastructure that works between RFPs.
